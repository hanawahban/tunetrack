# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single listener (this is a personal/learning project) tracking their own music listening habits — browsing a catalog of artists/albums/tracks, logging plays ("scrobbling"), and checking back on listening stats. Casual, personal use, not a multi-tenant SaaS audience.

## Product Purpose

TuneTrack is a lastfm-style scrobbling app: it tracks what you listen to and lets you browse your music collection. The catalog browse should feel like physically flipping through a record collection rather than querying a database — every album is a CD or vinyl object you pick up, not a row in a table. Success looks like: browsing feels tactile and enjoyable, scrobbling and stats stay fast and unobtrusive.

## Brand Personality

Dim, moody used-record shop. Warm low light, worn wood/crate textures, analog and a little dusty — the crate-digging feeling of flipping through a stack of records, not a retail showroom. Intimate and personal (a private collection), not commercial or polished-retail.

## Anti-references

Not a generic SaaS admin dashboard — no card-grid catalog view, no B2B-tool visual language regardless of mood. Also avoid the standard AI-slop defaults (cream/sand gradients, tiny uppercase eyebrows, numbered section markers, gradient text).

## Design Principles

- **Physical metaphor over UI convention** — browsing is flipping through crates/shelves, not scrolling a data table or card grid.
- **The metaphor must function, not just decorate** — CD/vinyl objects are real, clickable, navigable UI, not illustrative flourish on top of a normal list.
- **Warmth via material and light, not brightness** — dim, warm, low-contrast-background lighting carries the mood; not cheerful saturated color.
- **Collection intimacy over retail polish** — reads as one person's personal stash, not a storefront or commercial catalog.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: ≥4.5:1 body text contrast (including against the dim/low-light background — mood must not sacrifice legibility), full keyboard navigation for browsing and scrobbling, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for any shelf/crate motion.
